Moving Average (MA)

A moving average is a calculation to analyze data points by creating a series of averages of different subsets of the full data set.

It smooths out price data by creating a constantly updated average price.

Moving averages with a shorter look back period (20 days, for example) will also respond quicker to price changes than an average with a longer look back period (200 days).

- Short-term: 5, 10, 20 min/hour/day/week
- Medium-term: 50 min/hour/day/week
- Long-term: 100 és 200 min/hour/day/week

Types of moving averages

- Simple Moving Average (SMA): unweighted average of the previous k data-points.

- Exponential Moving Average (EMA): more weighting on the most recent price data.

Disadvantages

- It is calculated based on historical data, and not predictive.

- It works well in strong trending conditions but poorly in ranging conditions.

- It is best to utilize another indicator to help clarify the trend.

Fair Value Gap (FVG)

A price action concept used in ICT/SMC trading to identify price imbalances in the market.

Fibonacci retracement (Price pullback levels)

Horizontal lines on a stock chart that indicate where support and resistance are likely to occur. Fibonacci retracement levels are based on the Fibonacci sequence.

Elliott wave theory

The principle relies on analyzing price charts to identify wave patterns, which are fractal (self-similar patterns appearing at every degree of trend) in nature, meaning they repeat across different timeframes, and discern what prices may do next. It is about human psychology: market prices move in predictable, repeating waves.

Weaknesses of Indicators

EMA: It may generate false signals during sideways or choppy markets, periods of high volatility, or sudden price movements, as it reacts more strongly to recent price changes.
RSI: It does not perform well on lower timeframes (e.g., very short-term charts) as they become overly sensitive to price noise.
MACD: It gives unreliable signals in low-volume conditions due to reduced market participation.
