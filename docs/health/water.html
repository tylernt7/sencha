Pure Water: Deuterium-depleted water (DDW)

0. Purify the water

  • Leave the still mineral water or tap water for 3-4 hours (gases escape)
  • Boil the water
  • Cool the water
  • Filter the limescale using clean gauze
  • Keep the bottle in the freezer until 3/4 of it is frozen
  • Keep the frozen water (3/4), throw away unfrozen water (1/4)
  • Melt the ice at room temperature

1. Remove the first layer of ice

  • Take the purified water
  • Fill a PET/plastic bottle (no glass and metal) with water up to about 85-90% and close with a cap
  • Place the bottle in the freezer so that about 1/5 of it becomes ice (about 2-3 hours)
  • Keep the liquid water, throw away the frozen water

2. Form the ice

  • The remaining water begins to form the ice core. As the ice crystals start to form, they naturally push away all impurities and admixtures.

3. Remove the unfrozen water

  • The water that does not freeze because of impurities stays in a liquid form.

4. Melt the ice

  • The final layer of absolutely pure and crystal clean ice is melted back into water and bottled for your enjoyment.

5. Make crystal water

  • Put the deuterium-depleted water in a bottle and freeze it for at least 6 hours
  • The water after defrosting is crystal water

  • The melting point of deuterium is +3.8°C
  • Impurities freeze at -7°C
  • Do not use snow, because it contains a lot of dirt, heavy metals and harmful salts
  • Do not use chlorinated tap water, which is dangerous and can lead to cancer

  • Melt water preserves its good properties for 7 days, and loses it when heated above +40°C (better immediately at +20-25°C)
  • Melt water should not be boiled, because all beneficial properties are lost
  • No more than 30% of the total liquid volume can be meltwater (3-5 glasses)
